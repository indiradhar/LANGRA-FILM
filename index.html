Market Opportunity

India has never seen a mainstream feature film about para-athletics. With the success of Dangal (₹2000Cr+), Chak De India (₹100Cr+), and recently 12th Fail (₹70Cr+), audiences are hungry for authentic, inspirational stories rooted in Indian soil.

Lyangra fills a crucial gap by addressing disability representation in Indian cinema while delivering the emotional punch and crowd-pleasing moments that make sports dramas box office gold.

Universal Appeal

Every parent will see themselves in Malati. Every child who's been called "different" will see themselves in Suresh. This is India's story.

The Story

Act 1: Six-year-old Suresh is born with one leg shorter than the other in a rural West Bengal village where he's labeled "lyangra" (cripple). His father Ashok, pressured by village shame and his superstitious mother, rejects him. Only his mother Malati refuses to give up. When a progressive schoolteacher recognizes Suresh's raw speed despite his disability, secret training begins.

Act 2: By age 14, Suresh wins the state para-athletics championship and qualifies for nationals in Delhi. Enter Mr. Ranjan Bose, a guilt-ridden arms manufacturer seeking redemption after his factory's gun paralyzed a child. He becomes Suresh's sponsor. But at nationals, corrupt coach Vikram Malhotra sabotages Suresh's wheelchair on behalf of a wealthy rival's mother, causing him to finish 4th in the finals.

Act 3: CCTV evidence exposes the sabotage. Under massive public pressure, the race is re-conducted. Suresh wins with a national record time of 56.9 seconds. His father arrives seeking forgiveness, but faces the consequences of his absence. Suresh goes on to represent India at the 2000 Sydney Paralympics.

Themes & Emotional Core

Unconditional Maternal Love

Malati's journey from a guilt-ridden mother who defines herself through her son's success to learning that true love means acceptance. This emotional arc will resonate with every parent in India.

Redefining Disability

Suresh's transformation from a "broken" child seeking validation to a champion racing for self-worth challenges India's deep-seated prejudices about disability.

Rural India's Hidden Talents

A powerful statement about how India's villages harbor extraordinary potential that's crushed by poverty, superstition, and lack of opportunity.

What Makes This Story Different

Not a pity story: This is a high-stakes sports thriller with sabotage, corruption, and a climactic re-race that will have audiences on the edge of their seats.

Flawed, real characters: Malati isn't perfect—she pushes too hard. The father isn't purely evil—he's weak. These gray characters make the story authentic and relatable.

Visual spectacle: Wheelchair racing is cinematic gold—speed, danger, technique. Think Mad Max meets Chariots of Fire on an athletics track.

Unforgettable Cinematic Moments

The Bleeding Feet Reveal

After winning his first school race at age 10, Suresh removes his shoes to reveal bloodied, swollen feet—he's been running on his limp for years. The doctor's verdict: "If he continues, he'll lose function in his good leg." Suresh's breakdown and Malati's embrace will leave theaters in tears.

The Sabotage Discovery

Finals day, national championship. Suresh's wheelchair tires deflate mid-race. He finishes 4th, collapsing in tears. CCTV footage reveals Coach Vikram puncturing the tires at 11:37 PM. The entire nation watches the scandal unfold on live TV.

The Re-Race Climax

After a media storm and #JusticeForSuresh trending nationwide, the finals are re-conducted. Suresh unleashes "The Suresh Surge" in the final 100m—explosive double-push technique, 45-degree cornering. He breaks the national record: 56.9 seconds. The stadium erupts.

Father's Rejected Redemption

Ashok travels 24 hours by train to Delhi to congratulate his son. Malati's ice-cold response: "Where were you when he was bleeding?" Suresh's calm verdict: "You don't get to celebrate victories when you weren't there for the defeats." Ashok sits alone in the stadium, watching from afar.

Risk Mitigation Strategy

Identified Risks & Solutions

Risk: Child Actor Performance

Mitigation: Extensive 6-month training program, experienced acting coach, multiple child actors cast for different age ranges, rehearsal-heavy shooting schedule.

Risk: Sensitive Subject Matter

Mitigation: Disability rights consultants on payroll, sensitivity readers for script, partnerships with leading disability advocacy organizations, test screenings with differently-abled communities.

Risk: Budget Overruns

Mitigation: 10% contingency built in (₹4 Cr), detailed pre-production planning, experienced line producer, storyboard-driven shooting for efficiency.

Risk: Regional Language Limitation

Mitigation: Simultaneous Hindi dubbing, English subtitles for international markets, strategic release timing (Durga Puja for Bengal, Diwali for pan-India).

Risk: Racing Sequences Execution

Mitigation: Hire international para-athletics stunt coordinators, multiple camera setups, VFX backup for crowd enhancement, real Paralympic athletes as body doubles/consultants.

Risk: Marketing Reach

Mitigation: ₹15 Cr P&A budget, grassroots marketing through NGOs and schools, social media influencer partnerships, government promotional support.
